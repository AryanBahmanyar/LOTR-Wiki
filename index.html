Intro

The Lord of the Rings, also known as LOTR, follows the treacherous journey of the protagonist, Frodo Baggins, to defeat the Dark Lord Sauron, who is attempting to take over the realm of Middle-earth. To do so, he has to destroy Sauron's source of power, the One Ring, which Frodo has found himself to be in possession of. However, the ring can only be truly destroyed where it was created by Sauron himself; in the fires of Mount Doom, at the heart of Mordor, Sauron's pocket region of Middle-earth. To get to Mount Doom, Frodo has to travel all the way across Middle Earth from his small and quaint village, located in a peaceful region known as the Shire. The fantasy story was initially written as a novel by English author, J.R.R. Tolkien, between 1937 and 1949, and was published between 1954 and 1955. It had become one of the best-selling books of all time, with over a 150 million copies sold. The novel was later divided into the 3 following volumes:

  1. The Fellowship of the Ring
  2. The Two Towers
  3. The Return of the King

Movie Adaptations

The story was later adapted into a trilogy of long movies and is considered to be one of the most successful and influential movie series of all time. The movies were directed by Peter Jackson, who also later directed the Hobbit (another one of Tolkien's works) trilogy between 2012 and 2014. One movie from the trilogy was released each year over the course of 3 years, between 2001 and 2003. The movies were mostly shot in Jackson's homeland, New Zealand. The 3 movies combined were nominated for a total of 30 Academy Awards, 17 of which they won, most notably by The Return of the King. Collectively, the 3 movies accounted for a budget of $281 million and sold for more than $3 billion at the box office.

The Fellowship of the Ring

The Fellowship of the Ring is the first entry in The Lord of the Rings trilogy. The protagonist, Frodo Baggins, begins his journey when he inherits a mysterious and powerful object, known as the One Ring, from his uncle, Bilbo Baggins. Frodo, upon learning about the antagonist, the Dark Lord Sauron, and how the ring is his source of power, sets out on a journey to destroy the ring, so that Sauron cannot use it to dominate the realm of Middle-earth, the fictional land where the story takes place in. Frodo sets off from his small village in the Shire, a peaceful region of Middle-earth, along with his cousin, Pippin Took, his loyal gardener, Sam Gamgee, and their friend, Merry Brandybuck. Together, they set off towards a town to meet up with an old friend of Frodo and Bilbo; a wizard known as Gandalf the Grey. In their journey through a forest, the 4 hobbits see mysterious ghoulish men in black garments, riding black horses, known as the Nazg√ªl, the Ringwraiths; they are agents of Sauron looking for the ring. Avoiding detection, the hobbits make it to the village of Bree where Frodo was supposed to meet Gandalf, but they learn that Gandalf hasn't made it. Instead, the hobbits meet a mysterious human character, who goes by the name, Strider. Sauron who is aware of the ring's location due to his connection to it, sends his Ringwraiths to Bree to retrieve it. Strider, who is a friend of Gandalf, saves the hobbits and takes them towards an elven kingdom known as Rivendell. On their way, the Ringwraiths continue to pursue them, but Strider, along with an elven friend of his who meets up with them, manage to fend off the Ringwraiths; but not before Frodo gets injured and passes out. After waking up, Frodo finds himself in Rivendell where he sees both Gandalf and Bilbo again. After Bilbo sets off for good, Frodo is brought to the council of Elrond, the elven king. This is where the fellowship, a group dedicated to helping Frodo destroy the ring, is established. Here, Frodo along with Gandalf and his 3 hobbit friends, is joined by Legolas, an elven archer, Gimli, a dwarf, Boromir, a human warrior who's also an heir to Denethor II, the king of Gondor, and Strider, who is now revealed to be Aragorn, the heir of Isildur, an ancient king of Arnor and Gondor. Together, the fellowship sets off, travelling through dangerous snowy mountains in a blizzard, and through ancient dwarven mines, known as the Mines of Moria, while fending off Sauron's demonic foot-soldiers, known as orcs, as well as trolls and other hostile entities along the way. The story concludes in a region of Middle-earth, known as Amon Hen. Here, the fellowship is pursued by a stronger breed of orcs, known as Uruk-hai, who are soldiers of not Sauron, but Saruman, who is a powerful ally of Sauron. Merry and Pippin get taken away, Boromir is killed, and Gandalf was already lost to a demon, known as the Balrog, in the Mines of Moria, or so the fellowship thought. This only left Frodo and Sam who got broken up from the rest of the group and went in their own way, and Aragorn, Legolas, and Gimli, who went in another direction. The story concludes with the fellowship broken up, setting up the stage for the split groups of protagonists to experience different journeys in the next 2 stories.

The Two Towers

The second entry in The Lord of the Rings trilogy continues the journey of the protagonists who are now split into 3 groups. Frodo and Sam continue their journey to Mordor when they encounter Gollum; a disheveled creature who has a destructive obsession with the One Ring. Frodo and Sam do not trust Gollum, but without any sense of direction, they have to trust Gollum to guide them to Mordor. Aragorn, Legolas, and Gimli pursue the captured Merry and Pippin, when they come across soldiers of the kingdom of Rohan. They learn about the growing threat of war from Saruman, a powerful ally of Sauron, and attempt to help Rohan prepare for the coming invasion. Meanwhile, Merry and Pippin, who have escaped their Uruk-hai captors, have now ran into and got lost in a nearby forest, known as Fangorn Forest. While Frodo and Sam continue their perilous journey, Saruman's army marches towards Helm's Deep; a strong and strategic fortress in the kingdom of Rohan. By night, Saruman's forces arrive from Isengard, Saruman's region of Middle-earth, and start to attack Helm's Deep. Aragorn, Legolas, and Gimli, who are helping Gondor, are also fighting alongside the soldiers to defend the fortress. Meanwhile, in the Fangorn Forest, not too far away from the action at Helm's Deep, Merry and Pippin have encountered strange tree-like creatures, known as Ent. They speak with their leader, and convince him to attack Isengard. By dawn, as the war rages at Helm's Deep, the situation is looking desparate for the defenders. But then, out of nowhere, Gandalf appears with reinforcements and they cahrge at the invaders. With Gandalf's help, the defenders fend off Saruman's army and emerge victorious. Meanwhile, Merry and Pippin, along with their new Ent friends are marching towards Isengard and once they arrive, they start to wreak havoc. Aragorn, Legolas, Gimli, and Gandalf, who is now known as Gandalf the White, arrive just in time to see Saruman get killed by one of his own servants who felt mistreated. With the threat of Saruman neutralized, Gandalf, Aragorn, and the others now focus thier concern on Sauron's army. Meanwhile, Frodo, Sam, and Gollum get captured by Faramir and his men. Faramir, who is revelead to be Boromir's brother, is also tempted by the ring, just as Boromir was. However, unlike Boromir, Faramir resists his temptation and lets the 3 of them continue on with their journey. As Frodo and Sam get closer to Mordor, they start to see more warning signs from Gollum and his struggles to resist his urges to fight for the ring. The threat of the Ringwraiths, Sauron's ghoulish warriors, also increases as they continue their hunt for Frodo and the ring. The story concludes with Frodo and Sam, as weary as they are, growing more hopeful as they get closer to Mordor. The title of the story refers to the 2 towers, as in the tower of Sauron, Barad-d√ªr, and Orthanc, Saruman's tower, and the alliance between the 2 antagonists.

The Return of the King

The final entry of The Lord of the Rings continues the journey of the protagonists as they go on with their struggles against the forces of Sauron. Gandalf, along with Pippin, goes to Minas Tirith, the capital city of the kingdom of Gondor, to pay his debt to the king, Denethor II, for Boromir's sacrifice. Boromir, a respected warrior of Gondor, was Denethor II's eldest son, and heir to the throne. Aragorn, Legolas, and Gimli, knowing that Sauron could attack Gondor at any time, go to the Paths of the Dead; an ancient tomb that houses a long dead army, known as and their cursed spirits, known as the Dead Men of Dunharrow. They would not be free of their curse and rest in peace until they fulfilled their oath to fight for Gondor. The trio go there to seek the dead army's help in their fight against the forces of Sauron. The trio weren't successful at first and were met with hostilities from the spirits. However, Aragorn, who was revealed to be the true heir of Isildur, the long-dead king that placed the curse on the now dead army, has authority over the curse. Upon revealing his identity, he convinces the dead army to join him in his fight against Sauron. Meanwhile, Frodo, Sam, and Gollum continue on with their journey. They arrive at a dark city, known as Minas Morgul. The name has a resemblence to Gondor's capital, Minas Tirith, because it was once a city of Gondor, called Minas Ithil. However, it was conquered by Sauron's army long ago, and was renamed to Minas Morgul; the city was corrupted over time. With mountain ranges surrounding Mordor, there are only 2 entry and exit points, one of which is Minas Morgul. The trio, aware of the risks of passing directly through the city, go through a hidden passage around it, using Gollum's knowledge of the area. As they do so, they witness the city's gates open and Sauron's army marching out of it to head towards the capital of Gondor. Then trio then continue their journey through the treacherous mountain passage until Frodo and Sam walk into a the lair of a giant spider, known as Shelob. Gollum, who guided the 2 hobbits into the trap, disappears, leaving Frodo and Sam to confront Shelob. The 2 hobbits fight to defend themselves, but then Shelob bites Frodo, leaving him paralyzed with its venom. Sam continues to fight Shelob, and manages to drive the giant spider away. While Frodo is only paralyzed, Sam believes him to be dying and he carries Frodo, continuing their journey to Mordor. Meanwhile, Sauron's army has arrived at Minas Tirith and they've started to lay siege. A massive battle begins as the Siege of Gondor is initiated. Legolas, Gimli, Merry, Pippin, and Gandalf are also participating in the battle to defend the city. On the first day of the siege, the defenders struggle, but keep their ground. The siege continues overnight and by the next day, the invading forces break through the gates, but the defenders do what they can to hold them back. King Th√©oden of Rohan arrives with his army to help Gondor fight back against Sauron's forces. With his cavalry, they charge towards the invading army. However, not long after, king Th√©oden gets killed in the ensuing battle. Aragorn then arrives with the army of the Dead Men and with the combined forces of the 3 armies, they push Sauron's army back and emerge victorious. With their new victory, Aragorn and Gandalf, along with Legolas, Gimli, Merry, Pippin, and the combined armies of Gondor and Rohan, prepare to march toward Mordor to take the fight to Sauron. As they make their way to Mordor, the orcs prepare a new army to defend Mordor, and among them are Frodo and Sam, who under disguise, sneak through the chaotic crowd of orcs. As they make their way towards Mount Doom, Aragorn's newly assembled army arrives at the gates of Mordor; the main entry and exit point of the land. As the final battle begins, Frodo and Sam make their way up Mount Doom to destroy the One Ring once and for all. When they get close to the entrance that leads to the mouth of the volcano, Gollum appears again, attacking the 2 hobbits in a desparate attempt to take the ring before they destroy it. The hobbits manage to fend off Gollum long enough for Frodo to make it to the mouth of Mount Doom. With a little hesitation, Frodo drops the ring into the lava and Gollum leaps into the air to save the ring. However, the ring and Gollum both fall into the lava and the One Ring, Sauron's source of power, and the object that has tempted and corrupted many individuals, is finally destroyed for good. As a result, Sauron loses all his energy and the tower collapses, and while it does, the ground beneath his army also cracks and collapses, plunging his entire army of orcs into the deep abyss, symbolizing the end of all evil in the land. The story concludes with Aragorn, the heir of Isildur, being crowned as the new king of Gondor. Sam, Merry, Pippin return to their village in the Shire to celebrate, while Frodo, after his noble quest, sets off on a journey with Bilbo and Gandalf in a bittersweet farewell to his friends.

Legacy

Since their initial release, both Tolkien's novels and Jackson's movie adaptations have been inspirations for other Lord of the Rings movies and other media, including TV series, such as Amazon's 2022 series, The Lord of the Rings: The Rings of Power, as well as video games, such as Monolith Production's 2014 game, Middle-earth: Shadow of Mordor. Tolkien's work also inspired the popular table-top game, Dungeons & Dragons. Tolkien's work not only inspired other works of the high fantasy genre, but the very genre itself. It's no wonder why he's referred to as the "father of fantasy". The LOTR franchise has also inspired many talented people to create their own art, both visual and musical. Fun Facts

  1. In addition to the hundreds of millions of copies sold, the book has also been translated into at least 38 languages!
  2. The first movie is 178 minutes, or 2 hours and 58 minutes long, the second one just 1 minute longer, and third one is 201 minutes, or 3 hours and 21 minutes long. The total length adds up to 558 minutes, or 9 hours and 18 minutes!
  3. The first movie premiered at the Odeon Leicester Square in London, UK, the second at the Ziegfeld Theatre in New York City, and the third at the Embassy Theatre in Wellington, New Zealand.
  4. The 3 movies were mostly shot simultaneously from October 11, 1999 to December 22, 2000.
  5. All 3 movies won the Academy Award for Best Visual Effects, while none of them won the award for Best Supporting Actor. All the other awards were won by at least 1 of the movies from the trilogy.
  6. The Return of the King alone was nominated for 11 of the 14 Academy Awards and won all 11 of them!

  7. Bilbo Baggins is the protagonist of one of Tolkien's earlier works, The Hobbit, which LOTR is the sequel to. Peter Jackson later created a trilogy for The Hobbit as a prequel to his The Lord of the Rings trilogy, with 1 movie being released each year between 2012 and 2014. The 3 movies in order are:

    1. An Unexpected Journey
    2. The Desolation of Smaug
    3. The Battle of the Five Armies
